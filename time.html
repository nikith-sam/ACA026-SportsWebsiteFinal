How was Virat Kohli's life shaped?

  • 5 November 1988 Virat Kohli was born to a Punjabi Hindu family in Delhi.
  • 1998 Kohli joins the West Delhi Cricket Academy.
  • October 2002 Kohli played for Delhi Under-15 team in October 2002 in the 2002–03 Polly Umrigar Trophy. He was the leading run-scorer for his team in that tournament with 172 runs at an average of 34.40.
  • 2003Virat became the captain of the team for the 2003–04 Polly Umrigar Trophy and scored 390 runs in 5 innings at an average of 78 including two centuries and two fifties.
  • July 2006 Kohli was selected in the India Under-19 squad on its tour of England.
  • 19 December 2006 Kohli's father died on 18 December 2006 due to a stroke after being bed-ridden for a month.
  • February 2006 Kohli captained the victorious Indian team at the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup held in Malaysia.
  • 2008Following the Under-19 World Cup, Kohli was bought by the Indian Premier League franchise Royal Challengers Bangalore for $30,000 on a youth contract.
  • April 2011 Kohli played in every match of India's successful World Cup campaign.
  • March 2012 Kohli was appointed the vice-captain for the 2012 Asia Cup in Bangladesh on the back of his fine performance in Australia.
  • March 2013 Kohli started a charity foundation called Virat Kohli Foundation (VKF).
  • June 2013 Kohli featured in the ICC Champions Trophy in England which India won.
  • October 2016 Indian Player Virat Kohli became the first batsman in the history of Test cricket to score four double hundreds in four consecutive Test series.
  • 30 March 2017 Virat Kohli was awarded the Padma Shri award by President Pranab Mukherjee at a ceremony at the Rashtrapati Bhavan, which is the fourth-highest civilian award in the Republic of India.
  • 2017 Kohli was ranked 7th in the list released by Forbes as the Most Valuable Brand among athletes.
  • 25 September 2018 Virat Kohli was provided with the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna Award by President Ram Nath Kovind at the Rashtrapati Bhavan.
  • April 2019 Kohli was named the captain of India's squad for the 2019 Cricket World Cup. On 16 June 2019, in India's match against Pakistan, Kohli became the fastest batsman, in terms of innings, to score 11,000 runs in ODI cricket.Eleven days later, in the match against the West Indies, Kohli became the fastest cricketer, in terms of innings, to score 20,000 runs in international cricket, doing so in his 417th innings.Kohli scored five consecutive fifty plus score in the tournament.
